Did you know that the food supply chain contributes to roughly 25% of greenhouse gas emissions globally?

Awareness of our diet goes hand in hand with our awareness of climate change. According to the United Nations, global emissions much fall by 7.6% each year to meet the Paris Climate Agreement target if 1.5Â°C.

What is this tool?
This site is meant as an educational tool that translates food on your plate into average emissions produce to get to your plate.

Why Salad?
Salad has been chosen as we tend to equate salad as healthier and more 'plant-i-ful' and, therefore, better for the environment. However, how they're produced directly informs carbon intensity. Further, this tool will help you understand the effect of adding animal (by)products to your salad!

How does it work?
  1. Select your salad ingredients in the dropdown list below; You will see an ingredient pop up in the menu below. Each item selected is considered one serving per ingredient.

  2. Click on an ingredient to see more facts* about each one.

  3. As you select your ingredients, you will see the Carbon Counter* and the Percent of Fair Daily Food Emissions* rise.
*These data have been taken from My Emissions and are calculated based on global CO2 emissions from food procution. That means, your location and the specific local suppply chain are not taken into consideration.

What do these numbers mean?
The Carbon Counter

  • This data point is measured in 'CO2e' (Carbon Dioxide equivalent). That means that all greenhouse gases produced by making your meal were converted into CO2 as a measuring stick. Therefore, this measurement offers insight into the amount of CO2 equivalent emissions produced from your production to your plate.


  • Data are based on the global average CO2 production of food products. They, therefore, do not take into consideration local production of your meal. This, therefore, could lead to inaccuracies.



The Percent Daily Food Emissions

  • This data point shows the percentage of your salad's CO2e emissions measured against the quota of daily food emissions that should come from out meals.
  • The quota is 3.05kg CO2e per day. This daily amount is based on the UN recommended amount in order to limit global warming to 1.5 degrees celsuis.
