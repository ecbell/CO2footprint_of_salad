Did you know that the food supply chain contributes to roughly 25% of greenhouse gas emissions globally?

That said, awareness of our diet goes hand in hand with our awareness of climate change. According to the United Nations, global emissions much fall by 7.6% each year to meet the Paris Climate Agreement target if 1.5Â°C.

What is this app?
This app is meant as an educational tool that translates food on your plate into average emissions produce to get to your plate.

Why Salad?
Salad has been chosen as we tend to equate salad as healthier and more 'plant-i-ful' and, therefore, better for the environment. Although plants do tend to be easier on the environment, how they're produced directly informs carbon intensity. Further, this app will help you understand the effect of adding animal (by)products to your salad!

How does it work?
  1. Select your salad ingredients in the dropdown list below; you will see your dancing ingredient pop up in the menu below. Each item selected is considered one serving per ingredient. For a double serving, simply add another serving of the same ingredient.

  2. Scroll over each ingredient to see more facts* about each one.

  3. As you select your ingredients, you will see the Carbon Counter* and the Percent of Fair Daily Food Emissions* rise.
*These data have been taken from My Emissions and are calculated based on global CO2 emissions from food procution. That means, your location and the specific local suppply chain are not taken into consideration.
